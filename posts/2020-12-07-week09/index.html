    Week 09: P&L Statement

    Understanding the P&L Statement

    • Fundamental Analysts need to know how to read the P&L statement, not how to make it.
    • Excise Duty is levied only on Sale of Products not on Sale of Services.
    • Stock in trade refers to finished goods of previous financial year being sold in this financial year
    • Depreciation/amortization is incurred as an expense over long term.
    • Purchases of stock-in trade is the cost of purchase of the products manufactured in the last FY.
    • Changes in inventories of finished goods.

    Questions

    1. What is the Purchase of Stock-in trade and Changes in inventories of finished goods?
    2. Why is EPS calculated on the whole profit after tax and the shares outstanding in the market rather than normalizing it by the equity of shares in the market? E.g.: if the company has only 25% of equity shares in the market, then how to calculate EPS.
