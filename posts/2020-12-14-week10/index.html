    Week 10: Balance Sheet (Liabilities)

    Understanding the Balance Statement

    • Balance Sheet Eqn: Shareholder’s Equity = Assets - Liabilities.
    • Balance Sheet presents a bird’s eye view of the company’s state of financials over the years.
    • Shareholder Fund’s
      • Shareholder Capital = FV x No. of outstanding shares => useful for calculating # shares.
      • Reserve and Surplus
        • Profit (obtained from P&L statement) is added to the surplus.
        • Some % of the profile is provisioned to the Reserve.
        • Dividends are paid out of surplus.
    • Liabilities
      • Think about this way – if you buy a mobile phone on EMI (via a credit card) you obviously plan to repay your credit card company within a few months. This becomes your ‘current liability’. However if you buy an apartment by seeking a 15 year home loan from a housing finance coampany, it becomes your ‘non-current liability’.
      • Non-current Liabilities
        • Liabilities not settled within the current FY.
        • Long Term Borrowing
        • Deferred Tax Liability
        • Long Term Provisions
      • Current Liabilities
        • Liabilities settled within the current FY.
