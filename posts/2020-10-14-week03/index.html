    Week 03: IPO and Stock Market

    The IPO Markets

    • The promoter/early investors retain control over the valuation by going through a typical Series funding path instead of early IPO.
    • Sequence of events for an IPO.
    • Merchant Banker helps decide the price band of the stock.

    Takeaways

    • Companies go public because:
      • Provide an exit for early investors
      • Reward the employees via ESOPs
      • Branding and customer engagement
    • As a potential IPO subscriber/bidder, one should read the DRHP of the company.

    The Stock Markets

    • The main job of the stock market is to create a place for the buyer and seller to trade in stocks.
    • NASSCOM is the trade association of the IT industry.
    • Methods for calculating returns
      • Absolute Return
      • Compounded Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)
    • Holding period is the time period the buyer intends to hold the stock for - ranging from minutes to “forever”.

    Takeaways

    • The actions of the market participants is news and events driven - potentially perception based.
    • The transparency about the Last Traded Price is important to prevent stock price manipulation.
    • Use CAGR to normalize the investment return over the holding period.
    • Always use CAGR when you want to check returns over multiple years.
    • Use Absolute Return for a holding period of 1 year or lesser.
    • Traders and investors differ on 2 accounts - risk taking ability and the holding period.
