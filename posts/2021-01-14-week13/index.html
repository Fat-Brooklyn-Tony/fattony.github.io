    Week 13: Cash Flow Statement

    Understanding Cash Flow Statement

    • Every legal company has 3 activities:
      • Operating Activity: operations related to daily business operations.
      • Investing Activity: decreases cash, increases assets
      • Financial Activity: financial activity related to raising cash and redistributing cash.
    • Link between Cash Flow and Balance Sheet.
      • Cash Flow of the company = Cash and Cash Equivalents in Current Assets in Balance Sheet.
    • Whenever liabilities increase/decrease, cash balance increases/decreases.
    • Whenever assets increase/decrease, cash balance decreases/increases.
    • CF of the company = Net CF of the 3 activities.
    • CF statement has the 3 activities separated into segments.
    • Cash Flow of the company represents the actual liquid cash the company has in hand.
    • A company with a positive cash flow from operating activities is always a sign of financial well being.
